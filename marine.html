Marine and Beach Tourism

Indonesia’s marine and beach tourism offers world‑famous destinations like Bali’s Kuta Beach for surfing and sunsets, Komodo National Park for snorkeling and diving among vibrant coral reefs, and the Thousand Islands for tropical relaxation, boating, and swimming.

Kuta Beach

Kuta Beach is a famous 2.5-km stretch of cream-colored sand on Bali’s southwestern coast, known for its vibrant nightlife, popular surfing spots, and stunning sunsets.

Derawan Islands (East Kalimantan)

A remote archipelago famous for pristine beaches, manta rays, and green turtle nesting sites. It’s a paradise for divers and snorkelers seeking untouched coral reefs.

Komodo Island

Pink Beach, also known as Pantai Merah, is a stunning beach located in the Komodo National Park in Indonesia. The beach gets its name from the pink hue of its sand, which is caused by the crushed red corals mixed in with the white sand. Pink Beach is known for its crystal clear waters and breathtaking views.
