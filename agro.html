Agricultural Tourism

Agricultural/Agro tourism in Indonesia invites visitors to explore rice terraces, coffee plantations, and rural landscapes, offering a blend of natural beauty and cultural traditions. It highlights how farming and agriculture shape local communities while providing scenic and educational experiences.

Tegalalang Rice Terrace (Bali)

Famous for its dramatic, layered rice paddies in Ubud, this terrace showcases Bali’s traditional subak irrigation system and offers breathtaking views for visitors.

Jatiluwih Rice Terraces (Bali)

A UNESCO Cultural Landscape site, Jatiluwih features expansive rice fields that demonstrate sustainable farming practices and Bali’s centuries‑old agricultural heritage.

Kintamani Coffee Plantation (Bali)

Located near Mount Batur, this plantation lets visitors experience coffee cultivation firsthand, including the famous Balinese coffee varieties, while enjoying panoramic mountain views.
