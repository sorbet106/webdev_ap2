Shopping Tourism

Shopping tourism in Indonesia combines modern malls, bustling traditional markets, and specialty centers, offering visitors everything from luxury brands to local crafts and textiles. It reflects Indonesia’s vibrant urban culture and diverse economic life.

Grand Indonesia Mall (Jakarta)

One of the largest malls in Indonesia, Grand Indonesia offers international fashion brands, dining, entertainment, and cultural showcases, making it a premier shopping and leisure destination in the capital.

Tanah Abang Market (Jakarta)

Recognized as the biggest textile market in Southeast Asia, Tanah Abang is famous for its wide variety of fabrics, clothing, and wholesale goods, attracting both local buyers and international traders.

Pasar Beringharjo (Yogyakarta)

A historic traditional market located near Malioboro Street, Pasar Beringharjo is known for batik textiles, local crafts, spices, and traditional foods, offering visitors an authentic cultural shopping experience.
