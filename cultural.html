Cultural and Heritage Tourism

Cultural and heritage tourism in Indonesia highlights traditional arts, historic landmarks, and living customs that reflect the nation’s diverse identity. Visitors can immerse themselves in performances, architecture, and rituals that preserve Indonesia’s legacy.

Wayang Kulit (Central Java)

Traditional shadow puppet theater, recognized by UNESCO, where intricate puppets and gamelan music bring epic stories from the Ramayana and Mahabharata to life.

Batik Villages (Solo & Yogyakarta)

Centers of Indonesia’s batik tradition, where visitors can learn the art of wax‑resist dyeing and explore patterns that symbolize cultural values and regional identity.

Minangkabau Traditional Houses (West Sumatra)

Distinctive Rumah Gadang houses with sweeping horn‑shaped roofs, representing the matrilineal culture of the Minangkabau people and their architectural heritage.
