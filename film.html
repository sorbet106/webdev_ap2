Film Tourism

Film tourism in Indonesia highlights locations featured in international movies and shows, attracting visitors who want to experience the cinematic landscapes firsthand. From temples to tropical beaches, these sites blend culture, history, and natural beauty with global media exposure.

Lake Toba

Featured in Indonesian films and travel documentaries for its volcanic lake and Batak culture. Its scenic landscapes provide a cinematic backdrop for both drama and adventure genres.

Bali (Eat Pray Love, 2010)

Several scenes from the Julia Roberts film were shot in Ubud and Padang Padang Beach, showcasing Bali’s serene landscapes and spiritual atmosphere.

Borobudur Temple (The Fall, 2006)

This UNESCO World Heritage site in Central Java was featured in Tarsem Singh’s visually stunning film The Fall, highlighting its grandeur and cultural significance.
