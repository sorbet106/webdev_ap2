Historical Tourism

Historical tourism in Indonesia highlights districts, forts, and colonial buildings that reflect the nation’s past. These sites allow visitors to explore Indonesia’s cultural heritage, colonial history, and architectural legacy.

Kota Tua Jakarta (Jakarta)

A historic district filled with Dutch colonial buildings, museums, and old town squares. It offers a glimpse into Jakarta’s past as a bustling colonial trading hub.

Fort Rotterdam (Makassar, South Sulawesi)

A well‑preserved Dutch colonial fortress built in the 17th century. It served as a strategic stronghold and now houses museums showcasing Makassar’s history and culture.

Lawang Sewu (Semarang, Central Java)

A famous historic building from the Dutch colonial era, known for its “thousand doors” design. It is an architectural landmark and a symbol of Semarang’s colonial heritage.
