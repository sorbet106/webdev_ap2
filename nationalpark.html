National Park Tourism

National park tourism in Indonesia highlights the country’s extraordinary biodiversity and unique wildlife. These protected areas allow visitors to experience rare species, pristine ecosystems, and natural wonders while supporting conservation efforts.

Ujung Kulon National Park (Banten, West Java)

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Ujung Kulon is home to the critically endangered Javan rhinoceros. It also features lush rainforests, coastal scenery, and volcanic landscapes.

Tanjung Puting National Park (Central Kalimantan, Borneo)

Famous for its orangutan conservation programs, Tanjung Puting offers river cruises through dense jungle, where visitors can observe orangutans, proboscis monkeys, and diverse birdlife.

Gunung Leuser National Park (Sumatra)

Part of the Tropical Rainforest Heritage of Sumatra, this park is one of the richest ecosystems in Southeast Asia. It is home to Sumatran orangutans, elephants, tigers, and rhinoceroses, making it a vital conservation area.
