Wildlife Tourism

Wildlife tourism in Indonesia highlights encounters with rare and iconic species in their natural habitats. Visitors can explore national parks and islands where conservation efforts protect unique animals found nowhere else in the world.

Orangutans in Tanjung Puting National Park (Central Kalimantan, Borneo)

Famous for orangutan conservation, this park offers river cruises through dense jungle where visitors can observe orangutans, proboscis monkeys, and diverse birdlife.

Komodo Dragons in Komodo National Park (East Nusa Tenggara)

The world’s largest lizards, Komodo dragons, roam freely across the islands. Guided tours allow visitors to safely observe these prehistoric creatures in their natural environment.

Birdwatching in Bali Barat National Park (Bali)

A paradise for bird lovers, this park is home to the endangered Bali starling and over 160 other bird species, along with mangrove forests and coral reefs that enrich the ecosystem.
