Architecture Tourism

Architecture tourism in Indonesia highlights the country’s diverse cultural and religious heritage through iconic buildings, ranging from grand mosques to historic churches. These sites reflect Indonesia’s history, artistry, and unity in diversity.

Istiqlal Mosque (Jakarta)

The largest mosque in Southeast Asia, built to symbolize Indonesia’s independence. Its modern design blends monumental scale with Islamic tradition, and it stands across from Jakarta Cathedral as a symbol of religious harmony.

Gereja Blenduk (Semarang, Central Java)

A historic Protestant church built in 1753, known for its striking dome and colonial Dutch architecture. It is one of the oldest churches in Java and a reminder of Indonesia’s colonial past.

Masjid Raya Baiturrahman (Banda Aceh, Sumatra)

Originally built in the 17th century, this grand mosque is famous for its elegant domes and resilience. It survived the 2004 tsunami and has become a symbol of faith, strength, and Acehnese identity.
