Geological Tourism

Geotourism in Indonesia highlights the country’s dramatic volcanic landscapes and unique geological phenomena. Visitors explore active volcanoes, crater lakes, and rare natural wonders that showcase Indonesia’s dynamic earth heritage.

Mount Bromo (East Java)

One of Indonesia’s most famous active volcanoes, Mount Bromo offers stunning sunrise views over the Tengger Caldera and is a highlight for adventure seekers and photographers.

Mount Rinjani (Lombok, West Nusa Tenggara)

A massive volcano with a spectacular crater lake (Segara Anak), Mount Rinjani is popular for trekking and provides breathtaking views of Lombok’s rugged landscapes.

Mount Merapi (Central Java)

Known as Indonesia’s most active volcano, Mount Merapi combines geological significance with cultural importance. Its eruptions have shaped the surrounding land and traditions, making it a powerful symbol of resilience.
